## 3. Formulas (exact — no approximation)

- **Compound, fresh contributions:** `A = PMT × [(1+r/12)^(12t) − 1] / (r/12)`
- **Compound, existing principal P:** `A = P(1+r/12)^(12t) + PMT × [(1+r/12)^(12t) − 1] / (r/12)`
- **Boundary guard (fix D):** if `r == 0` → `A = P + PMT × 12 × t` (the limit; avoids /0). Also handle
  `t==0` → `A = P`, `PMT==0` → pure principal growth.
- **Inflation (real value):** `real = nominal ÷ (1.03)^t` (3% default; `economic_digest` may override).
- **Investable surplus:** `max(0, income − survival − fixed − debt_min − emergency_fund_contrib − goal_savings)`.
- **Allocations** round to 1 decimal and **must sum to 100 ± 1%**.

## 5. Investment-safety guardrail (Q5 — critical)

The skill provides **general** financial guidance. It MUST NOT:
- compare specific securities against each other ("buy AAPL over MSFT"),
- recommend buying/selling specific stocks based on quote data,
- present precise stock-vs-stock performance analysis off possibly-delayed Yahoo data.

It MAY: value the user's existing holdings (Σ shares×price, timestamped), suggest **broad**
diversification (VTI/VXUS/BND class), and rebalancing toward bracket allocation targets. When a user
asks for specific stock analysis, decline gently and offer the general alternative. Enforced at
N-PORTFOLIO-ENGINE (scope), N-ADVERSARIAL (challenge any specific-pick language), N-QUALITY-GATE
(block), and V-FIN-24.

## 6. Quote freshness policy (Q1/Q2/Q3 + fix E)

- Refresh a ticker if its cached quote is **>5 min old**; else reuse. Never re-fetch the same ticker
  >1×/60s globally.
- Every displayed price carries the **exact ISO8601 fetch timestamp** AND the quote's market
  `source_ts` (Yahoo daily/intraday stamp) — e.g. `VTI: $372.45 (fetched 2026-06-03T15:47:30Z; market data 2026-06-03T20:00:00Z)`.
- `>24h` old → **STALE** (red). yfinance failure/empty → **OFFLINE**: use cached price with a very
  explicit warning + the exact timestamp of the cached data. Note prices "may be delayed."

4. Investment split pie • 5. Compound growth line (nominal + real) • 6. Portfolio breakdown bar
